# EEAT Principles

Google's EEAT framework evaluates content quality across four dimensions: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. This assessment influences both SEO rankings and AI-generated answers.

## The Four Components

**Experience** emphasizes firsthand knowledge, demonstrated through personal anecdotes, case studies, tested results, and real screenshots rather than purely theoretical content.

**Expertise** requires subject matter knowledge signaled through credentials, comprehensive coverage, technical accuracy, and citations to authoritative sources.

**Authoritativeness** establishes recognition as a field leader through backlinks from respected domains, industry publication mentions, and consistent brand presence.

**Trustworthiness** centers on accuracy and transparency, requiring clear authorship, fact-checked information, HTTPS security, and visible contact details.

## Technical Implementation

The framework suggests organizing author data with fields capturing credentials, biographical information, and canonical profile URLs. Content documents should track publication dates, update timestamps, expert reviewers, and source citations to support EEAT signals throughout the site architecture.

## Special Requirements

Sensitive topics—medical, financial, legal, and safety-related content—demand heightened EEAT standards. This includes professional review, expert credentials, and appropriate disclaimers to establish legitimacy in high-stakes subject areas.
